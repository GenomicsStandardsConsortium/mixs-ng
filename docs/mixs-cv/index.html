Controlled vocabularies

Whenever possible, MIxS metadata descriptors contain a a controlled vocabulary (CV) of terms as the value of a descriptor. A CV is an organized arrangement of words and phrases used to index content and/or to retrieve content through browsing or searching. The purpose of CVs is to organize information and to provide terminology to catalog and retrieve information. CVs promote consistency in preferred terms and the assignment of the same terms to similar content. While this might be restrictive, experience has shown that such CVs vastly enhance searching and automated retrieval of genomic datasets, as opposed to free text values. The CVs are dynamic, and are extended when required and requested.
